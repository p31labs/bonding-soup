## Medium form (LinkedIn / Discord / Matrix — ~400–600 words)

---

**I've been building something.**

For 18 months — across a car, a Camden County parking lot, a VW Golf with spotty I-95 cell service — I've been quietly building P31 Labs.

P31 Labs is a Georgia nonprofit I incorporated in April 2026 with a mission: **Build, Create, Connect.** Specifically: build decentralized, open-source tools for neurodivergent families, and do it without surveillance-capitalism infrastructure underneath.

Here's what exists today:

**[Cognitive Passport](https://p31ca.org/passport-generator.html)** — a structured self-advocacy tool. It turns the thing AuDHD people have to re-explain to every new doctor, teacher, and social worker into a shareable, printable artifact. It runs entirely in the browser. Nothing is stored.

**[BONDING / family mesh](https://p31ca.org)** — a K₄ complete-graph communication mesh for separated families. Four vertices, six edges, no single point of failure. Built on Cloudflare Workers + Durable Objects. Zero monthly cost. Session-signed with Ed25519 keys. No behavioral data leaves the mesh.

**k4-agent-hub** — a four-node agent worker tetrahedron (ForgeHub, CounselHub, ScholarHub, ScribeHub) with dock protocol, anchor pacts, and federation dispatch. The skills layer for the mesh. Ships this month.

---

**Why I built this:**

I'm AuDHD, late diagnosis, 2025. I have hypoparathyroidism — a chronic calcium disorder where "low calcium" isn't a minor deficiency, it's a medical emergency. Executive dysfunction isn't a character flaw; it's a serialization bottleneck in a brain that works well when the interface is designed for it.

I have two kids — S.J. (9) and W.J. (6). We're navigating a custody situation. The communication tools that exist for separated families are either surveillance-capitalism products, expensive, or built for neurotypical workflows.

So I built the ones I needed. And then I open-sourced them, incorporated a nonprofit, filed for 501(c)(3) status, and got a SAM.gov UEI so I can apply for grants to keep building.
