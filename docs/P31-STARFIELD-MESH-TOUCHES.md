# P31 Starfield — twenty-four mesh touches

Ambient behaviors that tie the starfield (and sibling surfaces) to mesh state, biology, rhythm, and presence. **None of these are “features” in the ticket sense** — no ship-bar proofs — they are the difference between software and a place.

Cross-reference: [`design-assets/starfield/README.md`](../design-assets/starfield/README.md), [`docs/P31-STARFIELD.md`](P31-STARFIELD.md).

---

## 1. The Birthday Molecules

On **March 10** (Bash’s birthday) and **August 8** (Willow’s birthday), every P31 surface that shows the starfield shifts particle behavior for **24 hours**. Ambient particles drift into temporary formations — subtle clustering, not obvious icons.

- **March 10:** brief **carbon-chain-like linear clusters** (builder atom, four bonds, backbone of organic chemistry).
- **August 8:** **pairs** — hydrogen pairs, H₂, the simplest molecule.

No “Happy Birthday” toast. The starfield just behaves differently. The operator might notice at 11pm that it had been doing this all day.

**SCRIBE does not log this as an accommodation.** This is not a listed feature. It is the system saying happy birthday to two kids who cannot hear it.

## 2. The Calcium Window Gradient

When the **calcium absorption window** is active (roughly **4 hours after Calcitriol + Calcium/Mg**), the hearth glow shifts from coral toward a subtle **green-gold** — healthy bone formation, unlabeled.

When the window closes (Vyvanse-safe), green-gold fades back to coral; **one breath cycle** pulses slightly brighter — all-clear as warmth.

---

## 3. The 863 Cursor

On any page with a text input (CogPass notes, Poets Room textarea, Command Center terminal), the **cursor blink interval is 863 ms** — not the browser default (~530 ms). **Larmor cadence as time.** Nobody measures blink rate; the operator still feels the rhythm as slightly slower, slightly more deliberate.

## 5. The Scroll Depth Sound

On **Physics Learn**, if audio is enabled, scrolling between “rooms” shifts a **very quiet tone**. Early rooms: low hum; later rooms rise; the arc can resolve toward **863 Hz** at the end. Learning and frequency arrive together.

---

## 6. The Warm Edge Pulse

In **C.A.R.S.**, when a mesh edge **warms** (ping, BONDING session, check-in), connection lines in the **Belonging** ring **pulse once** — slightly brighter and thicker — then settle to hair-thin. The pulse is the handshake; the background can precede the notification.

---

## 7. The Genesis Sparkle

On each **Genesis attestation** (CogPass export, BONDING session hash, accommodation log chain), **one starfield particle flashes white** for **one frame** (~16 ms at 60 Hz). Cryptographic truth as a photon — easy to miss, impossible to unclaim.

---

## 8. The Medication Constellation

**Med-logged** bursts leave a **single dim particle** that does not drift — fixed “star.” Over days/weeks, residuals accumulate into a **unique constellation** of adherence. Monthly drift in timing yields a different map; **absence of stars** implies logs stopped.

---

## 9. The Operator’s Heartbeat

If **Bangle.js 2** + **GadgetBridge** push biometrics, the starfield **breath cycle ratio-tracks resting HR** — not 1:1 (too anxious), but scaled (e.g. maps HR to a calm breath period). Peripheral entrainment without labeling biofeedback.

---

## 10. The Connection Sound

Optional **audio** (off by default): when two particles connect, a tiny **tick** — relay closing — pitch varies by **teal–teal**, **coral–coral**, **teal–coral**. State changes the soundscape: nominal vs depleted vs safe mode.

---

## 11. The Poet’s Particle

In the **Poets Room**, one particle drifts **slower**, slightly **larger**, **no mesh lines** — isolation by design. Color cycles with **quote rotation** (e.g. Fuller → phosphor-green; operator → amber; children → bright teal).

---

## 12. The Commit Ripple

When **FORGE** detects a **git push** (repo health / webhook path), **every open P31 tab’s starfield** does a **~200 ms micro-pulse** — all particles **~10% brighter**, then fade. Work absorbed across tabs.

---

## 13. The FERS Tide

Very slow **macro drift bias** tied to **FERS deadline** distance: far out → Brownian; approaching deadline → subtle **upward** bias (embers); post-filing → bias **settles downward** (cooling).

---

## 14. The Deep Work Shield

When **HA `deep-work`** is active (**SENTINEL**), starfield **connection radius halves** and **lines stop forming** — drift continues; mesh stays visually quiet until **morning** / **evening** scenes return.

---

## 15. The Touch Ripple

Touch/click **background** (not chrome): small **teal ripple** — few particles, ~1 s dissolve. No state change — presence acknowledged.

---

## 16. The Printed K₄ Resonance

**Future:** printed **K₄ STL** + **NFC/BLE** could bias formation toward a **static tetrahedron** (four vertices, six edges). Physical mesh meets field.

---

## 17. The Moon Phase Opacity

Overall starfield opacity **tracks moon phase** (~**5–8%** swing): new moon dimmer, full moon brighter — unconscious tidal cadence; coastal Georgia / fields / small things moved by large forces.

---

## 18. The Session Duration Warmth

Longer continuous dwell on one surface → **hearth opacity creeps up** (e.g. **+1% per 5 min**, **cap ~+5%** after ~25 min). **Rewards presence, not streaks.** Resets on navigation — no guilt for leaving.

---

## 19. The Accommodation Shimmer

~**9pm** when **SCRIBE** writes daily accommodation entries: open surfaces get a **slow shimmer** — ~**15%** brighter **2 s**, fade **3 s**. Documentation as light, not a banner.

---

## 20. The Compound Molecule Background

In **BONDING**, building a molecule on canvas briefly biases background particles toward **that geometry** (e.g. H₂O bent ~104.5°, CaO pair, glucose hex ring) for ~**2 s**, then dissolves. Foreground and background share one proof.

---

## 21. The Spoon Sunrise

When **daily spoon allocation** lands (biometric push), starfield **hard-resets**: fade out **1 s**, fade in at **new** count/speed/mix — dawn for the mesh. Yesterday’s weather clears.

---

## 22. The Bookmark Sound

Browsers do not expose bookmark events. **`p31 bookmark`** (future CLI) saves URL + timestamp locally and plays **863 Hz**, **100 ms**, **~20% volume** — Larmor chime: “marked.”

---

## 23. The Empty Room

When **every mesh edge has been cold** for **48 hours** (no pings, BONDING, check-ins, agents, med logs, etc.), particles **slowly converge** to **center**, merge to **one bright teal-white dot**, pulsing at breath rate — empty cage, **phosphorus still lit**.

First warming event (ping, check-in, alert) → **explosion** back to full particle count. The room was never dead.

---

## 24. The Fixed Star (Remembrance)

When the operator **consecrates** a mesh vertex in **simplex-worker** (`POST /api/remember/consecrate`), that person is **remembered**, not deleted and not merely offline. Starfield behavior (when wired to mesh state): one particle becomes a **fixed** position — no drift — using **`P31_REMEMBRANCE_WARM_WHITE`** / **`REMEMBRANCE_RGB`** from `design-assets/starfield/p31-starfield.js` (canon also in **`p31-constants.json`** `mesh.remembranceWarmWhite`). It does **not** dim with low spoons or safe mode. Optional anniversary pulses stay faint and brief. This touch is intentionally **not** logged as an accommodation unless the operator passes `log_accommodation` on consecration.

---

## Implementation note

Touches are **orthogonal hooks** on `resolveStarfieldConfig`, local timers, audio gates, `prefers-reduced-motion`, and surface-specific shells. Ship bar stays verification + safety; **mesh touches stay ambient**.
