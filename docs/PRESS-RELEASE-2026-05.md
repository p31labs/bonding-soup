**P31 Labs Launches PHOS — Voice-First Navigation for Neurodivergent Families**

*Phosphorus31 Operating System uses information theory to route users in 4 questions instead of 20*

**Saint Marys, GA — May 4, 2026** — P31 Labs, Inc., a Georgia nonprofit organization (EIN 42-1888158), today announced the public launch of PHOS (Phosphorus31 Operating System), a voice-first, inference-driven navigation system designed specifically for neurodivergent operators and their families.

## The Problem with Current Systems

Traditional navigation assumes users have infinite executive function — the ability to parse menus, remember hierarchies, and make sequential decisions without cognitive overload. For the 15-20% of the population who are neurodivergent (AuDHD, autism, anxiety disorders), this assumption creates barriers.

"I built P31 because I couldn't find tools that worked for my brain," said the founder of P31 Labs. "With hypoparathyroidism and late-diagnosed AuDHD, I have about 3 'spoons' of executive function per day. Most systems assume I have 30. PHOS assumes I have 3 and builds accordingly."

## The SIC-POVM Solution

PHOS applies a principle from quantum information theory called SIC-POVM (Symmetric Informationally Complete Positive Operator-Valued Measure). In quantum mechanics, this represents the minimum set of measurements needed to completely characterize a quantum state.

Applied to human-computer interaction: PHOS routes any user to their destination in 3-4 questions (d² where d=2 dimensions: self vs. family vs. professional vs. crisis). This is not UX guesswork — it's information-theoretic optimization.
