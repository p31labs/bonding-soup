# `_archive/brain-dumps/`

Large speculative AI-elaborated documents whose original operator insights got buried under scaffold during conversation capture.

## What lives here

The full raw transcripts of brain-dump sessions where signal-to-noise was very low (e.g. 2,941 lines reduced to 3 sentences). Triage records sit alongside the dumps as `.triage.md` siblings.

## Why kept in git, not deleted

Provenance. A future agent — or future operator — needs to be able to trace where a concept originated. If the dump is out of git entirely, the chain breaks: parking-lot row → "see archive" → 404. With the archive present, the trail is complete: raw material → triage → salvaged concepts → destination in canon.

## Why excluded from public surfaces

Public-voice verifiers and `build:doc-index` skip `_archive/**`. These documents are operator/agent infrastructure, not story.

## Negative-example value

A future reader who sees a 2,941-line brain dump reduced to 3 sentences learns something operationally important about signal-to-noise discipline. That's worth preserving.

## Contents

> When a brain-dump gets archived: drop the raw markdown here as `YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md`, and put the triage record (what was salvaged, what was rejected, three-sentence reduction) at `YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.triage.md` alongside it. Then add or update rows in `docs/PARKING-LOT.md` pointing at the salvaged concepts.

(Initial population of this directory is operator-driven — ready for the first dump.)
