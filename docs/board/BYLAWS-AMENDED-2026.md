## ARTICLE I — NAME AND PRINCIPAL OFFICE

**§1.1 Name.** The name of the corporation is P31 Labs, Inc.

**§1.2 Principal Office.** The principal office of the corporation shall be located within the State of Georgia at such address as the Board of Directors may designate. The corporation may have offices in such other places as the Board may designate.

**§1.3 Registered Agent.** The corporation shall maintain a registered agent and registered office in Georgia as required by O.C.G.A. § 14-3-501 et seq.

---

## ARTICLE II — PURPOSES AND LIMITATIONS

**§2.1 Purposes.** The corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, including: building accessible technology for neurodivergent individuals and families; supporting open-source research and development in affective computing and cognitive accessibility; and providing educational resources related to the intersection of neurodiversity, technology, and civil rights.

**§2.2 Mission Statement.** Build, Create, Connect.

**§2.3 Limitations.** No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of its purposes. The corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under IRC § 501(c)(3).

---

## ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP

**§3.1 No Members.** The corporation shall have no members. All rights customarily held by members shall be vested in the Board of Directors.

**§4.8 Resignation.** Any director may resign at any time by written notice delivered to the Chair or Secretary. Resignation takes effect upon receipt unless a later date is specified.

**§4.9 Removal.** A director may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds vote of the remaining directors at any regular or special meeting, provided at least 10 days' notice of the removal vote has been given.

**§4.10 Compensation.** Directors shall serve without compensation. Directors may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their service, subject to the organization's expense reimbursement policy.

## ARTICLE V — MEETINGS OF THE BOARD

**§5.1 Annual Meeting.** The Board shall hold at least one annual meeting each calendar year for the purpose of electing officers, reviewing the prior year's activities, approving the budget, and transacting such other business as may come before the Board.

**§5.2 Regular Meetings.** The Board shall hold regular meetings quarterly (at minimum). Meeting dates shall be set at the annual meeting.

**§5.3 Special Meetings.** Special meetings may be called by the Chair, the CEO, or any two directors upon not less than 5 days' written notice.

**§5.4 Notice.** Notice of meetings shall be given by email, text, or other electronic means. Notice shall include the date, time, location or video link, and agenda of the meeting.

**§5.5 Quorum.** A majority of the directors then in office (not fewer than 2) shall constitute a quorum. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned.

**§5.6 Action by Majority.** Except as otherwise required by law or these bylaws, all Board actions require the affirmative vote of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present.

**§5.7 Action Without Meeting.** The Board may take action without a meeting if all directors entitled to vote on the action consent in writing (including by email). Such written consent shall be filed with the minutes of the Board.

**§5.8 Remote Participation.** Directors may participate in meetings by telephone, video conference, or other electronic means by which all participants can simultaneously hear each other. Such participation constitutes presence in person for quorum and voting purposes.

**§5.9 Executive Sessions.** The Board may meet in executive session (without officers who are not directors) to discuss matters including legal proceedings, personnel matters, or any item where a conflict of interest exists.

## ARTICLE VI — OFFICERS

**§6.1 Officers Required.** Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 14-3-840 (as amended by 2023 Ga. Laws 260), the corporation shall have the following officers:

- **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** — sometimes referred to as President
- **Secretary**
- **Chief Financial Officer / Treasurer**

The CEO and Secretary **must be different individuals**. One person may hold any other combination of offices.

**§6.2 Current Officers.**

|--------|--------|-------|
| CEO / Chair | Will Johnson | Founder |
| Secretary | Joseph Tyler Cisco | Appointed Board Meeting #001 |
| CFO / Treasurer | Brenda O'dell | Appointed Board Meeting #001 |

**§6.3 Election and Term.** Officers shall be elected by the Board at each annual meeting. Officers serve one-year terms and may be re-elected without limit.

**§6.4 CEO Duties.** The CEO shall:
- Serve as the principal executive officer of the corporation
- Preside at Board meetings (or delegate to Chair if roles are separated)
- Execute contracts, agreements, and legal documents on behalf of the organization as authorized by the Board
- Provide a written or verbal report to the Board at each regular meeting
- Manage the day-to-day operations of the organization

**§6.5 CEO Spending Authority.** The CEO may approve expenditures of up to **$5,000** per transaction without prior Board approval. Expenditures above $5,000 require Board authorization. The CEO shall report all expenditures at the next regular Board meeting.

**§6.6 Secretary Duties.** The Secretary shall:
- Record minutes of all Board meetings and written consents
- Maintain custody of the corporate seal (if any) and corporate records
- Give or cause to be given all required notices of meetings
- Maintain the official register of directors and officers

**§6.7 CFO/Treasurer Duties.** The CFO/Treasurer shall:
- Maintain or oversee the maintenance of accurate financial records
- Present a financial report at each regular Board meeting
- Ensure compliance with all financial reporting requirements
- Oversee the preparation of the annual Form 990-N (or 990/990-EZ if applicable)

**§6.8 Removal.** Any officer may be removed by majority vote of the Board at any regular or special meeting.

## ARTICLE VII — SPECIAL PROVISIONS

**§7.1 Conflict of Interest.** Directors and officers shall annually execute the organization's Conflict of Interest Policy disclosure form. Any director with a material interest in a transaction before the Board shall disclose the interest and recuse themselves from deliberation and voting.

**§7.2 Founder Protection.** Any Board action that would commit organizational assets to a legal settlement or consent agreement affecting Will Johnson's ongoing family court proceedings (Camden County Superior Court Case 2025CV936 or any successor proceedings) shall require:
- At least 5 days' advance written notice to all directors
- The affirmative vote of a majority of **independent directors** (directors who are not Will Johnson)
- Written documentation of the basis for the action filed with the corporate records

This provision may not be amended without the written consent of Will Johnson for so long as he remains a director.

**§7.3 Document Retention.** The corporation shall maintain records in accordance with its Document Retention Policy, adopted by the Board, as amended from time to time.

**§7.4 Indemnification.** The corporation shall indemnify its directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by O.C.G.A. § 14-3-851 et seq. This indemnification covers acts taken in their official capacity in good faith in a manner they reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the corporation.

**§7.5 Insurance.** The Board may authorize the purchase of directors and officers (D&O) liability insurance as the Board deems appropriate.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — FISCAL YEAR AND FINANCES

**§8.1 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the corporation shall end on **December 31** of each year.

**§8.2 Banking.** The CEO is authorized to open and maintain bank accounts on behalf of the corporation. All accounts shall be in the legal name of P31 Labs, Inc. (EIN 42-1888158).

**§8.3 Checks and Transfers.** Checks, drafts, or other disbursements of $5,000 or more shall require the signature or electronic authorization of the CEO plus acknowledgment by one other officer.

**§8.4 Audit.** If the corporation's gross receipts exceed $500,000, the Board shall engage an independent CPA to perform a financial review or audit as required by applicable law.

---

## ARTICLE IX — AMENDMENTS

**§9.1 Amendment Procedure.** These bylaws may be amended at any regular or special meeting of the Board by a two-thirds vote of the directors then in office, provided that:
- The proposed amendment has been submitted to all directors in writing at least 7 days before the meeting; or
- All directors waive the notice requirement in writing.

**§9.2 Founder Consent Required.** Amendments to §7.2 (Founder Protection) require the written consent of Will Johnson for so long as he remains a director.

---

## ARTICLE X — DISSOLUTION

**§10.1 Dissolution Procedure.** The corporation may be dissolved upon a two-thirds vote of the Board. Upon dissolution, after paying or making provision for all liabilities, the remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations described in IRC § 501(c)(3), as the Board shall determine, consistent with O.C.G.A. § 14-3-1401 et seq.
