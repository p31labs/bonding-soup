## Purpose

This policy establishes procedures for setting and approving compensation for the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) and any other officers or key employees of P31 Labs, Inc. These procedures are designed to ensure that all compensation is reasonable, based on comparability data, and documented in a manner that meets the requirements of the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness under IRC §4958 and satisfies the IRS Form 1023-EZ review standard.

## Rebuttable Presumption Procedure (IRC §4958)

To establish the rebuttable presumption that compensation is reasonable, the Board shall:

### Step 1 — Comparability Data

Before approving any non-zero compensation, the Board or a designated Compensation Committee shall obtain and document at least **three (3) data points** from comparable organizations, including:

- Compensation surveys published by guidestar.org, Charity Navigator, or GuideStar Compensation Report
- IRS Form 990 compensation data from at least 2 organizations with similar mission, size, and geography
- Published salary surveys for the role in question

Comparable organizations are defined as: Georgia-based nonprofits, or national nonprofits in the civic-tech / neurodiversity / accessibility space, with gross receipts within 3x or 1/3x of P31 Labs' projected annual revenues.

### Step 2 — Independent Review

Compensation decisions must be approved by a **majority of disinterested directors** — directors who have no financial conflict of interest in the compensation being approved.

Will Johnson shall recuse himself from any vote regarding his own compensation. No vote shall take place while Will Johnson is present in the meeting room (or virtual call) during deliberation.

### Step 3 — Documentation

The Board shall document in meeting minutes (or written consent):
- The comparability data reviewed
- The names of all directors who voted, abstained, or recused
- The outcome of the vote
- The basis for the Board's finding that the compensation is reasonable

This documentation shall be prepared **no later than the date of the next Board meeting** following the vote and retained for at least 7 years.

## Compensation Components Covered

This policy applies to all forms of compensation, including:

- Base salary or stipend
- Health insurance or wellness reimbursements
- Retirement contributions
- Performance bonuses
- Deferred compensation
- Expense reimbursements above IRS accountable plan rules

Expense reimbursements following an IRS accountable plan (business purpose documented, receipts submitted within 60 days, excess returned) are **not** compensation and are not subject to this policy.

---

## Conflict of Interest

Any director, officer, or key employee with a financial interest in a compensation decision must:

1. Disclose the interest in writing on the current year's COI disclosure form
2. Recuse themselves from deliberation and vote
3. Not be present during the vote

---

## Annual Review

The Board shall review CEO compensation (even at $0) at least once per year as part of the annual meeting agenda. The review shall be documented in minutes.

---

## Amendments
