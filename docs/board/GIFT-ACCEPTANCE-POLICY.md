# GIFT ACCEPTANCE POLICY
## P31 Labs, Inc. — Georgia Nonprofit Corporation

**Adopted:** ___________________________ (Board Meeting #001 or Written Consent)  
**EIN:** 42-1888158  
**Effective:** Upon adoption by the Board of Directors

---

## Purpose

This policy governs the acceptance, valuation, acknowledgment, and use of all gifts received by P31 Labs, Inc. It protects the organization, donors, and the public by ensuring gifts align with the organization's mission and do not create undue burdens or liabilities.

---

## Gifts Accepted Without Board Approval

The CEO may accept the following gifts without prior Board approval:

| Gift Type | Channel | Limit |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Cash | Direct | Any amount |
| Credit/debit card | Stripe | Any amount |
| Recurring supporters | Ko-fi | Any amount |
| ACH / bank transfer | Mercury | Any amount |
| Check payable to P31 Labs, Inc. | Mail / in-person | Any amount |
| Gift cards or prepaid cards | Any | Up to $500 |

All gifts accepted under this section shall be recorded in the accounting system within 5 business days of receipt.

---

## Gifts Requiring Board Approval (Threshold: $5,000+)

Any single gift with a fair market value of **$5,000 or more** — regardless of form — requires **majority Board approval before acceptance**.

The CEO shall present the gift to the Board with:
- Donor identity (may be kept confidential from public records)
- Estimated fair market value and basis for valuation
- Any conditions or restrictions attached
- Any known conflicts of interest

The Board may accept, decline, or accept with modified conditions.

---

## Digital Assets and Cryptocurrency

P31 Labs, Inc. accepts cryptocurrency and digital asset donations with the following conditions:

1. **Accepted currencies:** Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), and stablecoins (USDC, USDT) as of the date of this policy. The Board may expand this list by resolution.
2. **Conversion:** All digital assets shall be converted to USD via a recognized exchange within **72 hours** of receipt unless the Board specifically votes to hold.
3. **Valuation:** Fair market value is the spot price at the time of conversion, documented by exchange receipt.
4. **IRS Form 8283:** Required for donor gifts of digital assets over $500. Organization shall sign IRS Form 8283 acknowledging receipt; organization does not represent a value.
5. **Donor-advised fund transfers** of digital assets follow standard DAF gift acceptance rules.

---

## Restricted Gifts

The organization may accept gifts restricted to a specific program or purpose if:

1. The restriction is consistent with the organization's mission
2. The CEO (for gifts under $5,000) or Board (for gifts $5,000+) determines the organization can fulfill the restriction
3. The restriction is documented in writing with the donor before acceptance

Gifts that cannot be used within 3 years of acceptance due to restriction shall be returned or the restriction renegotiated with donor consent.

---

## Gifts Declined (Without Exception)

The organization shall not accept:

- Gifts conditioned on any political endorsement or candidate support
- Gifts that would require the organization to act contrary to its stated mission
- Gifts from anonymous sources above $500 without additional documentation
- Gifts of real property without prior independent appraisal and Board approval
- Planned giving (bequests) commitments without a dedicated planned giving policy in place

---

## Donor Acknowledgment and Receipts

### IRS-Required Written Acknowledgment

For any single gift of **$250 or more**, the organization shall provide a written acknowledgment that includes:

- Organization name and EIN (42-1888158)
- Date of gift
- Amount of cash gift or description of non-cash gift (no dollar value for non-cash)
- Statement: "No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution" (or description of any goods/services and their value if applicable)

Acknowledgments shall be sent within **30 days** of the gift.

### Recurring Donors (Ko-fi / Stripe)

Monthly recurring donors shall receive:
- Automated receipt at each transaction via platform
- Annual summary letter by January 31 of the following year

### Quid Pro Quo Disclosures

If a donor receives goods or services in exchange for a contribution (e.g., merchandise, event tickets), the organization shall disclose the fair market value of the benefit received and the deductible portion.

---

## Donor Privacy

Donor names and contact information are **confidential** and shall not be sold, traded, or disclosed to third parties without written donor consent, except as required by law (e.g., IRS Schedule B for gifts ≥ 2% of gross receipts, filed under seal).

The organization's public transparency page (phosphorus31.org/transparency) will disclose aggregate fundraising totals but not individual donor names without permission.

---

## Valuation of Non-Cash Gifts

| Gift Type | Valuation Method |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Publicly traded securities | Closing price on date of transfer |
| Cryptocurrency | Spot price at time of conversion to USD |
| Software licenses | Vendor's stated fair market value (not promotional price) |
| Professional services | Not deductible; do not record as revenue |
| In-kind goods | Donor-provided FMV, subject to reasonableness review |

---

## Amendments

This policy may be amended by majority vote of the Board of Directors at any duly noticed meeting.
