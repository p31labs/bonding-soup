## Purpose

P31 Labs, Inc. is committed to operating in conformance with applicable laws and ethical standards. This policy is intended to encourage and enable directors, officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors to raise good-faith concerns about illegal or unethical behavior without fear of retaliation.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers of P31 Labs, Inc.

---

## Reporting Concerns

Any person covered by this policy who has reasonable cause to believe that P31 Labs, Inc. or a person acting on its behalf has engaged in conduct that is illegal, fraudulent, or in violation of its policies is encouraged to report the concern promptly.

**How to report:**

1. **To the President:** will@p31ca.org
2. **To the Secretary:** [Brenda O'Dell contact — store off-repo]
3. **To the full Board:** report in writing addressed to all three directors

If the concern involves the President, report directly to the Secretary or another director.

**Anonymous reporting:** Concerns may be submitted anonymously by mailing a written statement to the principal office (401 Powder Horn Rd, Saint Marys, GA 31558), addressed to "Board of Directors — Confidential."

---

## Non-Retaliation

P31 Labs, Inc. will not retaliate against any person who reports a concern in good faith. Retaliation includes, but is not limited to: termination, demotion, suspension, harassment, or any other adverse action against the reporting person.

Any director, officer, employee, or contractor who retaliates against a good-faith reporter is subject to disciplinary action, up to and including removal from their position.

This protection does not extend to persons who make reports they know to be false.

---

## Investigation

The Board (or a designated disinterested director) will investigate all good-faith reports promptly and in a confidential manner. The investigating party will:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the report within 10 business days.
2. Conduct a reasonable investigation.
3. Report findings to the full Board.
4. Take corrective action where warranted.

---

## Confidentiality

Reports and investigations will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law and consistent with a thorough investigation. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed without consent, except where disclosure is required by law.

---

## Legal Protections

This policy does not limit any protections available to reporters under federal or Georgia law, including Sarbanes-Oxley Act protections for nonprofit employees who report fraud on federal agencies, and any applicable Georgia whistleblower statutes.

---

## Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed by the Board annually, at the annual meeting. The Board may amend this policy by majority vote at any duly convened meeting.
