# ASAN Teighlor McGee Disability Justice Mini-Grant
## 500-Word Narrative — P31 Labs, Inc.

**Operator:** William R. Johnson  
**Organization:** P31 Labs, Inc. (Georgia 501(c)(3), EIN 42-1888158)  
**Amount Requested:** $6,250

---

I am AuDHD (autistic with ADHD) with hypoparathyroidism. Late diagnosis, 2025. I have two children, ages 9 and 6, in a custody situation that means most of my communication with them happens through FaceTime calls scheduled by someone else, supervised by someone else, surveilled by someone else.

The tools that exist for separated families assume neurotypical workflows. They assume verbal back-and-forth is easy. They assume you can keep track of three different court-ordered apps without a cognitive prosthetic. They assume you have the executive function to initiate a phone call, maintain the thread of conversation, remember what was said, and follow up.

None of those assumptions hold for me. Or for my kids.

So I built what I needed. I built BONDING, a chemistry game that lets me play with my children remotely without giving Meta or Google behavioral data on my kids. It passes 424 automated tests and runs entirely on serverless infrastructure — no backend to maintain, no company to keep alive. I built the Cognitive Passport, a structured self-advocacy tool I use in doctor's appointments and court hearings when my verbal processing collapses under stress. It encodes my medical history, communication preferences, and accommodation needs into a portable document I can hand to anyone. I built PHOS, a voice-first interface that adapts its complexity to my available cognitive energy — at full spoons it shows animations and ambient audio; at zero spoons it reduces to black and white with a single breathing guide.

All of this runs on $0/month of serverless infrastructure because I cannot afford a cloud bill. The constraint of zero budget forced a design that doesn't depend on a company staying solvent. It depends only on math, cryptography, and open protocol.

P31 Labs is now a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with IRS determination as of May 2026. We have 22 peer-reviewed publications on Zenodo with DOIs. We have a full 17-service DePIN aggregation stack called CashPilot that turns residential bandwidth into grant funding. We have a live cryptographic mesh at the edge, handling family communication with a zero-surveillance data model.

This grant would fund the physical layer I cannot self-fund. It would buy a Home Assistant host for local-first home automation — lights that dim when spoons are low, reminders that check calcium levels. It would buy a Bangle.js 2 wearable that streams heart rate variability data into the spoon management system, letting the software adapt before I crash rather than after. It would buy two tablets for my children so they can have mesh-scoped device identities without commercial accounts — no Google, no Apple ID required. It would fund provisional patent filings for the HERALD tomography algorithm and the Node One hardware prototype, protecting the IP so it can remain open source without being exploited. It would pay for DOBE certification so we can access supplier diversity channels as a disabled-owned business.

The $500 operator stipend represents 20 hours of paid work at $25/hour. I am currently at $0 income, pending FERS disability retirement determination from the federal government. Every hour I spend on grant applications is an hour I don't spend building. This stipend acknowledges that time has value even when the builder is disabled.

P31 Labs exists because I needed tools that didn't exist. I believe other neurodivergent families need them too. This grant would let me finish the physical layer, protect the intellectual property, and continue building in public for the commons.
