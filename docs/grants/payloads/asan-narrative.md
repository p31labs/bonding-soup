# ASAN Teighlor McGee Disability Justice Mini-Grant
## 500-Word Narrative — P31 Labs, Inc.

**Operator:** William R. Johnson  
**Organization:** P31 Labs, Inc. (Georgia 501(c)(3), EIN 42-1888158)  
**Amount Requested:** $6,250

---

I am AuDHD (autistic with ADHD) with hypoparathyroidism. Late diagnosis, 2025. I have two children, ages 9 and 6, in a custody situation that means most of my communication with them happens through FaceTime calls scheduled by someone else, supervised by someone else, surveilled by someone else.

The tools that exist for separated families assume neurotypical workflows. They assume verbal back-and-forth is easy. They assume you can keep track of three different court-ordered apps without a cognitive prosthetic. They assume you have the executive function to initiate a phone call, maintain the thread of conversation, remember what was said, and follow up.

None of those assumptions hold for me. Or for my kids.

So I built what I needed. I built BONDING, a chemistry game that lets me play with my children remotely without giving Meta or Google behavioral data on my kids. I built the Cognitive Passport, a structured self-advocacy tool that I use in doctor's appointments and court hearings when my verbal processing collapses under stress. I built PHOS, a voice-first interface that doesn't require reading social cues from a screen.

All of this runs on $0/month of serverless infrastructure. The constraint of zero budget forced a design that doesn't depend on a company staying solvent. It depends only on math, cryptography, and open protocol.

P31 Labs is now a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with IRS determination as of May 2026. We have 22 peer-reviewed publications on Zenodo. We have 424 automated tests across our software suite. We have a live K₄ mesh running at the edge, handling family communication with zero surveillance data model.

This grant would fund the physical layer I cannot self-fund: a Home Assistant host for local-first automation, a Bangle.js 2 wearable for HRV-based spoon management, tablets for my children so they can have mesh-scoped device identities without commercial accounts. It would fund IP protection for the HERALD tomograph algorithm and the Node One hardware prototype. It would fund DOBE certification so we can access supplier diversity channels.

The $500 operator stipend represents 20 hours of paid work at $25/hour. I am currently at $0 income, pending FERS disability retirement determination from the federal government. Every hour I spend on grant applications is an hour I don't spend building. This stipend acknowledges that time has value even when the builder is disabled.

P31 Labs exists because I needed tools that didn't exist. I believe other neurodivergent families need them too. This grant would let me finish the physical layer, protect the intellectual property, and continue building in public for the commons.
